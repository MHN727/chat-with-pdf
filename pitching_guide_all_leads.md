# 🔷 UNIVERSAL FIRST MEETING FRAMEWORK

Before we go lead-by-lead, here is the **general formula** for every first meeting:

## Step 1: Walk In (Don't Call First)
- In Bannu, **face-to-face** works 10x better than phone or email
- Dress professionally but not too formal — you want to look approachable
- Best time: **11 AM to 1 PM** (after morning rush, before lunch)
- Bring a **laptop or tablet** loaded with competitor examples

## Step 2: Start With Respect, Not Sales
- Greet them properly. Use "Assalam u Alaikum" and show respect
- **Don't start selling immediately.** First ask about their business
- Say: *"I have been hearing great things about your business. I wanted to learn more about what you do."*
- Let them talk for 3-5 minutes. **Listen carefully.**

## Step 3: Ask the Magic Question
- After they finish, ask: *"Do people ever search for your business on Google? Do you know what they see?"*
- Then **open Google on your laptop** and search for their business name
- Show them what comes up (usually nothing, or very little)
- Say: *"See? Your customers are looking for you, but they can't find you."*

## Step 4: Show Competitor Examples
- Open **2-3 competitor websites** you have prepared
- Say: *"Look at what your competitors are doing. They are getting customers that could be yours."*
- Let them feel the urgency naturally

## Step 5: Present Your Solution
- Say: *"I can build you a website that does everything they have — and more. And since I am local in Bannu, you can meet me anytime."*
- Show a **quick mockup or example** from your portfolio
- Give them a **printed one-page proposal** with package and price

## Step 6: Close the Deal
- Don't pressure. Say: *"Take your time. But our early-bird pricing ends this month. I can only offer this rate to the first 5 clients."*
- Leave your **business card** and a printed brochure
- **Follow up after 2-3 days** with a WhatsApp message
